🔎 Welcome, Heat Detectives!

🔬 BIG IDEA:
Different materials conduct heat at different rates!
🎯 OUR CLASSROOM MISSION:
Work together in teams to compare heat transfer along spoons made of different materials (metal, wood, and plastic)

📌 What's Different About This Activity?

  • 🤝 TEAM WORK - Everyone has a job to do!
  • 👀 YOU do the work - Students measure butter and handle timers
  • ⏱️ You start the timer - Not the teacher!
  • 📝 You record the data - In your team's table
  • 🌡️ You check the temperature - Every 2 minutes!
🥄🥄🥄 + 👥👥👥 = 🔬

🎮 Today's Activity

We will work in GROUPS to put butter on different spoons, start timers, and see which one melts the butter fastest! The faster the butter melts = the better that material conducts heat!

🤖 Heat Bot Says:
"Metal is like a highway for heat - it moves SUPER fast! Wood is like a slow path - heat takes its time. Plastic is in the middle!"

👥 Team Roles - Who Does What?

🎯 We Have 4 GROUPS in Our Class!

Each group has a special job to help the experiment work.

📋 Group Jobs

🧈 GROUP 1: Butter Team

Job: Measure and put 2g of butter/wax on each spoon

Tools: Butter, kitchen scale, spoons

When: At the start of the experiment

⏱️ GROUP 2: Timer Team

Job: Start stopwatch when spoons go in water, stop when butter melts

Tools: Stopwatches, timers

When: During the experiment

📝 GROUP 3: Recording Team

Job: Record start time, stop time, and alert when 2 minutes pass

Tools: Data tables, pencils, watch

When: Throughout the experiment

🌡️ GROUP 4: Temperature Team

Job: Check and record water temperature every 2 minutes

Tools: Thermometer, temperature log

When: Every 2 minutes during experiment

🔔 REMEMBER: All groups work TOGETHER! The Timer Team tells the Recording Team when to write. The Recording Team tells the Temperature Team when to check!

⚠️ Safety First - Important!

🛑 Follow These Rules:

  1. Hot water is 60°C - It can burn! Stay back from the saucepan.
  2. Teacher handles the hot water - Students observe from their seats, then assist with butter and timers only.
  3. Wear safety goggles - Protect your eyes!
  4. No touching hot items - Use the tools provided. Teacher uses heat gloves.
  5. Keep workspace clean - Wipe up any spills.
  6. Ask for help - If you're unsure about anything, ask the teacher!
  7. Work in your group - Stay with your team assigned area.

🧪 The Heat Transfer Experiment - Guided Steps

📦 Materials for Each Group

  • 🥄 3 Metal Spoons
  • 🥄 3 Wooden Spoons
  • 🥄 3 Plastic Spoons
  • 🧈 Butter or Wax (2g each)
  • ⚖️ Kitchen Scale
  • ♨️ Hot Water (60°C) - Teacher's
  • 🌡️ Thermometer
  • ⏱️ Stopwatch/Timer

📋 Experiment Steps - Let's Do This Together!

1
Butter Team prepares the butter
🧈 STUDENT JOB: Measure exactly 2g of butter using the scale. Put butter on the end of each spoon.
2
Teacher places spoons in hot water
👨‍🏫 TEACHER JOB: Carefully put all spoons into the hot water at the same time.
3
Start the timers!
⏱️ STUDENT JOB (Timer Team): Press START on your stopwatch the moment spoons go INTO the water!
4
Watch and wait!
👀 ALL STUDENTS: Watch the butter carefully. Don't blink!
5
Stop the timers!
⏱️ STUDENT JOB (Timer Team): Press STOP the moment you see the LAST bit of butter melt!
6
Record the time
📝 STUDENT JOB (Recording Team): Write the time in your table. Tell Temperature Team: "2 minutes coming up!"
7
Check temperature every 2 minutes
🌡️ STUDENT JOB (Temperature Team): Look at the thermometer. Write the temperature in your log every 2 minutes!
8
Try again!
🔄 STUDENT JOB: Repeat steps 1-7 for Trial 2!
